3. Holistic thinking ()
Statement Plan for achieving SSC’s targets over individual needs Rationale • Business value is gained when holistic service delivery is prioritized over individual mandates by shifting from isolation and control to collaboration and sharing • Organizational cost savings can be achieved when thought is put into potential uses and impacts beyond immediate needs • Solutions that consider the interdependencies between strategic goals, information systems and processes will deliver greater overall value in the long-run • Improved collaboration and decision making can be achieved when data from different sources is managed and structured consistently Implications • Additional effort is required to address broader challenges outside the needs of a specific initiative • Compromises may be introduced when thinking holistically • When thinking holistically business processes may require optimization • A holistic approach to stewardship and product management may require organizational change management and frameworks for ensuring consistency
