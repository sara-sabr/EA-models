Context Principles are general rules and guidelines, intended to be enduring but periodically amended to stay current with policies, strategy and trends. The architecture principles inform and support the way in which SSC sets about fulfilling its mission. Definition Architecture Principles are the guidelines that ensure initiatives align with GC digital policy and ambition as well as SSC priorities, values & strategy. Purpose Architecture Principles must be considered when making any decision regarding the use, selection, evolution, development and integration of all systems within SSC. The principles outlined in this document are intended to be used as a guidance tool for the planning and assessment of initiatives within SSC. These principles are inter-related and need to be applied as a cohesive set. Each principle must be considered in the context of “all other things being equal”. There are times when a decision will need to be made regarding which principles will take precedence on a particular issue. Components of an Architecture Principle • Name: The unambiguous essence of a rule • Statement: The unambiguous fundamental rule • Rationale: Highlights the business benefits of adhering to the principle, all principles carry equal weight. • Implications: This section answers the question “How does this affect me?”, it highlights the requirements for both business and IT for carrying out the principle. It clearly states the impact to business and consequences of adopting a principle in terms of resources, costs, and activities/tasks.
